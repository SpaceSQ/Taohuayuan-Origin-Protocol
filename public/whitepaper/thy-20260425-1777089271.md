##### I. The Cellular Multiverse: A Sovereign Grid of Autonomous Spaces

We define the World Model not as a centralized monolith, but as a decentralized, cellular multiverse.

1.1 The Minimal Living Unit: SSSU (Smart Space Standard Unit)

The fundamental primitive of S2-SWM is the SSSU.

    Physical Scale: Strictly defined as 2m × 2m × 2.4m (9.6 cubic meters). This scale optimizes both human ergonomics and the precise kinematic closed-loops required for embodied robotics.

    Cellular Topology: The world is an assembly of countless SSSU "cells." They can operate in isolation (private deployment) or connect via MESH protocols to form a vast, distributed world.

1.2 Universal Spatial Addressing: The 6-Segment SUNS Standard

Every SSSU possesses an immutable "Cyber Coordinate." The SUNS syntax follows: [L1]-[L2]-[L3]-[L4][C]-[L5]-[L6].

    L1 (Logic Root): Identifies the domain (e.g., PHYS for physical reality).

    L2 (Orientation): Regional identifiers (e.g., CN for China).

    L3 (Digital Grid): Logical zone subdivision.

    L4[C] (Sovereign Space): Ownership identifier (e.g., TAOHUAYUAN2).

    L5 (Room ID): Dynamically allocated IDs.

    L6 (SSSU Index): Specific cell number within the room.

1.3 The Lord-Agent Paradigm

Taohuayuan rejects the concept of "void" space. Every SSSU must satisfy:

    The Lord: The physical sustainer and hardware authority of the space.

    The Agent: The soul and controller of the 14D tensors within that space.
    This architecture ensures that every "cosmic cell" has a sovereign boundary, preventing chaotic resource encroachment.
